# GuestBook Demo — Guided Tour

Five minutes, in the order that tells the story.

## 1. The landing page

You arrive on the sales page. **Launch the demo** (any of the green buttons) drops you into
the product. The **About** button in the demo's top bar brings the landing page back.

## 2. Analytics — the money

The default screen. Look for:

- **KPI strip** — Revenue, Occupancy, ADR, RevPAR with deltas vs. the previous period.
- **Revenue & Trends** — stacked by channel. Future months show "on the books" at lower opacity.
- **Channel Intelligence** — net revenue after commission per channel, and what commission
  actually cost this month.
- **Occupancy Calendar** — 26 weeks, GitHub-style. Hover any cell.

## 3. Compliance — the moat

Sidebar → **Compliance**. The modules card shows the engine: Austria and Italy active,
Germany and Switzerland as pluggable modules. Below it, the registration queue — every
arriving guest with their paperwork status and calculated local tax.

## 4. Inbox + Messages — the guest side

**Inbox**: three conversations across Booking.com, WhatsApp and Airbnb. Click between them.

**Messages**: the AI draft queue. One draft waits for approval (confidence 0.84), two went
out automatically (≥ 0.90). Note the drafts match the guest's language — the Müller reply
is in German.

## 5. Best Clients → the scraper

**Best Clients** ranks repeat guests by lifetime value. **✦ Find similar clients** on any
card jumps straight into the Marketing Hub's lead scraper, which finds local clubs and
companies that book stays.

## 6. Everything else

- **Ops Cockpit** — today's decisions in one list.
- **Marketing Hub** — leads, email campaigns (opens/clicks/bookings), scraper runs.
- **Pricing** — a price calendar with pace-based weekend suggestions.
- **Integrations / Settings** — every card and control responds.
- **EN / DE** — top bar, switches the interface language.
- **✦ (bottom right)** — the AI Command Centre. Ask it anything; demo answers are canned,
  the real product queries your live booking database.

## What the demo is not

No real data, no live connections, no account system. It demonstrates the interface and
the workflow — the product behind it runs on a real PMS sync, a real database, and a real
message pipeline.
